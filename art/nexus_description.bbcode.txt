[size=6][b][color=#e8905a]MLOX Subset Sort[/color][/b][/size]
[i]Sort your custom mods into your OpenMW load order — without ever touching your curated Modding-OpenMW.com list.[/i]

[line]

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]The problem[/color][/b][/size]
You built a carefully-tuned mod list from [url=https://modding-openmw.com/]Modding-OpenMW.com[/url] with umo + MOMW Configurator. Then you added a few mods of your own. They need to slot into the load order — but running a whole-list sorter like PLOX would scramble the curated MOMW order, which MOMW [b]explicitly warns against[/b].

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]What it does[/color][/b][/size]
MLOX Subset Sort treats your existing [font=Courier New]openmw.cfg[/font] order as [b]frozen[/b] and only works out where [i]your[/i] additions belong — using the real mlox rules — then writes the result back as a corrected [font=Courier New]momw-customizations.toml[/font].

That's the durable fix: it [b]survives every future Configurator rebuild[/b], and your curated list is [b]never reordered[/b]. Only your mods move.

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]Features[/color][/b][/size]
[list]
[*][b]Frozen curated order[/b] — sorts only your custom additions; the MOMW list is left exactly as it is.
[*][b]Real mlox engine[/b] — ported from mlox and cross-checked against plox: [font=Courier New]*[/font]/[font=Courier New]?[/font] wildcards and the [font=Courier New]<VER>[/font] token, ordering that bridges over plugins you don't have, and [font=Courier New][Conflict]/[Requires]/[Note][/font] warnings that understand [font=Courier New]ALL/ANY/NOT/DESC[/font] plus the [font=Courier New][VER]/[SIZE]/[DESC][/font] functions.
[*][b]plugin-order.yml aware[/b] — with your list name set, it tells curated-list plugins apart from your true additions and flags [color=#ffb454]redundant[/color], [color=#ffb454]orphan[/color], [color=#ffb454]needs-cleaning[/color], and base-order-drift issues.
[*][b]Conflict detection[/b] — find records two mods both touch, drill into a [b]field-level diff[/b], and see which plugin wins. Compiled scripts are [b]disassembled[/b] rather than shown as base64, so a script edit reads as the change it actually is.
[*][b]Cell map[/b] — a heatmap of which mods touch which cells, rendered in-app or in your browser.
[*][b]Resource (VFS) conflicts[/b] — the loose-file "Data" conflict view for your data paths, in load order.
[*][b]Health checks[/b] — master presence/order verification, a lint pass, save-file checks and timestamped backups.
[*][b]tes3cmd frontend[/b] — clean plugins without leaving the app (Morrowind/Tribunal/Bloodmoon are never touched).
[*][b]One-click mods-folder scan[/b] — point it at your custom mods folder and it builds the subset for you.
[*][b]Drag-and-drop GUI + full CLI[/b] — reorder rows, with a live, colour-coded log ([color=#7ee0a0]green = inserted/moved[/color], [color=#ffb454]orange = heads-up[/color], red = error).
[*][b]Fully themeable[/b] — [color=#bd93f9]Dracula[/color], [color=#a6e22e]Monokai[/color], [color=#61afef]Atom One Dark[/color], [color=#fabd2f]Gruvbox[/color] built in, and you can import your own or any [b]base16[/b] scheme. Themes the whole app, and switching applies instantly to every open window.
[*][b]Opt out what you don't want[/b] — disable plugins/paths (multi-select); anything already in your cfg is removed durably via [font=Courier New]removeContent[/font]/[font=Courier New]removeData[/font].
[*][b]Safe by default[/b] — dry-run preview, timestamped backups, and it can either emit a corrected TOML (recommended) or patch [font=Courier New]openmw.cfg[/font] directly.
[*][b]Cross-platform[/b] — Windows, Linux, macOS. Pure Python + tkinter.
[/list]

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]New in 3.0[/color][/b][/size]
[list]
[*][b]Theming for the whole app[/b] — the theme picker was log-panel-only; it now drives every window, button, frame, tab and list, and re-themes anything already open the moment you switch. Import your own theme as JSON or drop in a base16 scheme; a commented [font=Courier New]theme_template.json[/font] ships alongside to start from.
[*][b]The engine was rebuilt as a proper package[/b] — the monolithic script is now split by concern (rules, sorting, cfg handling, plugin metadata, downloads, script decoding). This is the invisible half of the release, and the important one: separating the pieces exposed code that was written but never wired up, and [b]that is where several real bugs were hiding[/b]. Most notably, [font=Courier New][SIZE][/font]/[font=Courier New][DESC][/font] rules were claiming matches for plugins that aren't on disk — which could fire a [font=Courier New][Conflict][/font] or [font=Courier New][Requires][/font] warning that was simply wrong.
[*][b]Verified, not asserted[/b] — 717 automated tests, plus a differential baseline pinning 41 behaviours against a real 687-plugin load order, so the refactor could be made without taking sorting behaviour on trust.
[/list]

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]Requirements[/color][/b][/size]
[list]
[*]OpenMW with a MOMW list installed via the [url=https://modding-openmw.com/guides/auto/]Automatic Install Guide[/url] (umo + MOMW Configurator).
[*]Python 3.10+ with tkinter (bundled with the python.org installers on Windows/macOS; on Linux e.g. [font=Courier New]sudo apt install python3-tk[/font]). [i]Not needed if you use the standalone .exe.[/i]
[*]mlox rule files ([font=Courier New]mlox_base.txt[/font] / [font=Courier New]mlox_user.txt[/font]).
[*][i]Optional:[/i] MOMW's [font=Courier New]plugin-order.yml[/font], and PyYAML (a built-in parser is used if it's absent).
[/list]

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]How to use[/color][/b][/size]
See the included [b]QUICKSTART[/b] for the 5-minute version. In short:
[list=1]
[*]Point it at your [font=Courier New]openmw.cfg[/font] and add [font=Courier New]mlox_base.txt[/font] + [font=Courier New]mlox_user.txt[/font].
[*]Build your subset: click [b]Scan...[/b] and pick your custom mods folder (or load your [font=Courier New]momw-customizations.toml[/font]).
[*]Set your [b]list name[/b] (e.g. [font=Courier New]total-overhaul[/font]) and point [b]plugin-order.yml[/b] at MOMW's file.
[*]Click [b]1. Sort[/b], review the panels and log, then [b]2. Export[/b] a corrected [font=Courier New]momw-customizations.toml[/font].
[*]Drop that file next to your [font=Courier New]openmw.cfg[/font] and re-run MOMW Configurator. Done — your mods sort into place on every rebuild.
[/list]

[size=5][b][color=#e8905a]What it will NOT do[/color][/b][/size]
[list]
[*]Reorder your curated MOMW list — ever.
[*]Write anything until you tell it to (dry-run is on by default; backups are made first).
[*]Guarantee your customizations are conflict-free. It helps you [i]see[/i] and [i]place[/i] them — the rest is on you. Customizations are not supported by the MOMW team.
[/list]
